Who is running for Mayor in New York City?

By Olivia Bensimon, March 9, 2021

Here we are, a little over three months out from the June primary where New Yorkers will all but elect the next mayor, and there are many candidates to think about. Here, you can use the drop down menus to filter candidates per issue they stand for.

It’s important to note that a majority of the candidates are running as Democrats (19 of them here, to be exact) and that sorting them along issues is not easy, and that the short bios are not enough to make a decision! The filtering is not perfect. I encourage you to click on the candidates’ image where you’ll be redirected to their online platform to learn more about them and what they propopse as mayoral candidates.

This is the first iteration of this project and will refocus the filtering as we get closer to the primary election.

Eric Adams

Says the city is mismanaged: is the guy to fix it. Policies supported by data. Supports small businesses. Pro-development. COVID-19 recovery is a priority: envisions revamping education, public health and public safety in the city. Former police officer, pro-reform/not “defund the police.” Currently Brooklyn Boro Prez.

Scott Stringer

Big theme: Rebuilding. Better public health structure and expand access to care. Small business support and more jobs. Investing in early childhood education. Improve transportation. Create a “Green New Deal” for NYC to address climate change. Affordable housing and end to homelessness. Currently City's comptroller.

Maya Wiley

COVID-19 Recovery. Police reform. Focus on inequality. Plans to address racial justice, education equity and school desegregation. “New Deal” for jobs and economic development. Plans to end evictions. Affordable housing and universal rent protection. Civil rights attorney and former Mayor Bill de Blasio counsel and MSNBC legal analyst.

Ray McGuire

Economic recovery: job creation, small businesses, innovation. Public Safety and restructuring the NYPD. “Cradle to career” model: investing in childcare and education. Affordable housing. Renovating NYCHA buildings. Supportive services for in shelters and services for people experiencing homelessness. Former vice chairman at Citigroup.

Andrew Yang

Cash relief and COVID-19 recovery. Universal Basic Income. Proposes city control of the subways. “Civilian Commissioner of the NYPD.” Small business relief. Specialized schools and broadband internet. Environmental justice and climate resiliency. Data driven approach to governance. Former Democratic presidential candidate in 2020 election and former nonprofit executive.

Shaun Donovan

The “15-minute neighborhood.” Innovation. Jobs. Better bus system. Public schools for all, by tackling damages from COVID-19 and preexisting inequities. Environmental justice and a sustainable city. Health equity and access to public health. Affordable Housing and addressing homelessness. Criminal and racial justice. Former HUD Secretary under President Barack Obama, and former HPD Commish' under Mayor Michael Bloomberg.

Dianne Morales

“Politics for all the people.” Values a care economy. Housing for all. Housing to end homelessness. Desegregating schools. Mental health services. Defunding the police and redistributing funds to social services to underserved communities of color. Workers rights. Small business recovery. Immigrant protection. Public Infrastructure investments. Wealth Tax. Affordable mass transit. Green jobs. Former social services nonprofit executive.

Kathryn Garcia

Government efficiency. COVID-19 Recovery. “Excellent basic services.” Fighting climate change. Composting Wants to create a pipeline between university and jobs. Free childcare for lower-income families. Affordable housing. Universal broadband. Police reform and increased implicit bias training. More bus and bike lanes. Former DOS Commish’.

Carlos Menchaca

Supporting NYC families. Investing in green technologies for new jobs. Universal Basic Income. Affordable housing. Funding public housing renewal and development. Equitable healthcare and mental health services. Expanding bus lanes and inclusive mass transit. Bike lanes. Allocating funds to social services, rethink NYPD. Desegregating schools. Currently a City Council member from Brooklyn.

Loree Sutton

“Quality-of-life” mayoral candidate. Economic Development and recovery. Mitigate threats caused by climate change. In-class education and technology. Accessible healthcare and robust mental health assistance. Affordable housing and options for those experiencing homelessness. Sanctuary City and immigration reform. Modernize mass transit. Former Veterans Affair Commish’.

Sara Tirschwell

“Rockefeller Republican.” Getting crime under control. More police presence. Community-police partnerships. Infrastructure projects. Restore all cleaning and sanitation services. Efficient governance. Remove bureaucracy and raise standards of excellence in schools. Better living conditions for people living in NYCHA. Increasing affordable housing stock. Kendra’s Law and sites with professional psychiatric services. Former Wall Street exec. CFO of mental health nonprofit.

Barbara Kavovit

Reform and rebuild NYPD – hiring more police officers, train them in community policing approach. Change bail requirements. Supporting Subsidizing small businesses loans and reform zoning and permit laws. Increase experience requirement for teachers. Better technology in schools, more afterschool programs. Tax breaks to businesses who build affordable housing. Inclusive healthcare systems. Emphasis on Women and LGBTQ issues. Appeared on RHONY. Founder of a commercial construction firm.

Curtis Sliwa

Law-and-order candidate. Tough on crime. Opposed to cutting funds from NYPD. Told the Daily News his priorities were more public bathrooms in the city and an end killing of animals in shelters. Founder of Guardian Angels. Radio host.

Fernando Mateo

Small business support. Fighting crime, emphasis on public safety. Hiring more police officers and restoring funding to the NYPD. Supports protecting landlords from tenants refusing to pay their rent. Spox for the New York State Federation of Taxi Drivers and United Bodegas of America.

Isaac Wright Jr.

Increase minimum wage, expand unionization, access to mass transit, affordable housing for working class New Yorkers. NYC should manage public transportation. Build affordable housing on empty city owned land. Reform policing, retrain to focus on de-escalation, create mental health responses separate from NYPD. Criminal justice. Desegregate schools. Refocus budget. Lawyer. Inspiration behind ABC legal drama “For Life” about his false conviction in 1991.

Art Chang

Universal child care. Stop evictions, invest in truly affordable housing. Support small businesses. Redirect police funds to social services, reimagine policing, change emergency response methods. End carbon emissions and prevent future climate damage. Efficient governance, bring to the 21st century with technology. Support the art and entertainment industry. Reframe educational priorities. Equality across all gov agencies. Co-creator of NYC votes.

Joycelyn Taylor

Reinvest in public housing. Create a path for ownership for NYCHA residents. Defund police, redistribute to social services. End broken windows policing. Funding for small businesses. End cash bail. Fund programs to reduce recidivism. Homeless and mental health services. Raise teacher salaries. Build a better public health program, expand NYC Cares. Sustainable city. Electric buses. Climate coastal resiliency projects. Legalize marijuana, expunge criminal records for people convicted of marijuana-related crimes and lower barriers for reentry. Founder of MWBE Alliance.

Aaron Foldenauer

Revitalize. Attract tourists and workers. Protect small property owners. Address food deserts, promote healthy eating. Sustainable environmental policies. Modernize infrastructure. Small business support. Police reform, demilitarize NYPD. Longer school year, charter schools, special ed reform. Land use and preservation. Reform public housing, incentivize building more rental housing. Solutions for homeless, reduce income inequality. Address aging New Yorkers and disabled. Lawyer.

Quanda Francis

Improve quality of life for all. Chief Equity Officer to analyze data from city agencies. Access to affordable housing. Equitable and affordable healthcare. Investing in digitalization of government services. Improve education for students with disabilities. Maternal health for women of color. Engage with the youth population, expand summer youth programs and internships. Repairs and renovations of NYCHA buildings. Currently president of a FinTech firm. Former crime analyst.

William Pepitone

Law-and-order candidate. Go back to “broken windows policing.” More cops on subways. Make more arrests. Remove homeless shelters from residential areas. End bail reform. “Pragmatic Governance.” Testing for specialized high schools. Charter schools. Reopening businesses. Reinvest in industry jobs. New construction and infrastructure. Former NYPD officer.

Christopher Krietchman

Provide basic human needs, food, water, hygiene, shelter, safety. Public health and wellness. Community and culture. Transparency in government management. Innovation and entrepreneurship by nurturing collaboration between various communities. Increase use of technology in education, encourage more job training and creation. Sustainable infrastructure. Affordable housing. Better access to funding for small businesses. Health+Wellness entrepreneur, former bodybuilder.

Paperboy Prince

Universal Basic Income. Medicare for all. “Democracy dollars” or a $500 stipend from feds and state gov for citizens to donate to political campaigns, goal to encourage more political participation. Spreading love, to strengthen community ties, establishing community centers to encourage discussion and entrepreneurship. Rapper. Former congressional candidate.

Eddie Cullen

Public-Private Partnerships. Public safety. Capitalism. Co-founder of Harlem Tech Summit.
